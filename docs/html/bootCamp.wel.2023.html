Neat NoteTaking

So, how will you participate during lectures at City?
What will you do in class?

It surprises me that some students seem to think that just sitting there will somehow give them the knowledge that they need. It doesn't - you need to think, answer questions, code and take notes. You need to engage and act and enquire to learn.

The Cornell Notes method for note-taking in lectures is worth considering.
The approach has helped some students in the past and seems useful to me.

It is explained in two short videos on the The Cornell Note Taking System. They are worth a look.

Now #BootCamp lectures are a little different to some other lectures, and you will have laptops out and are likely to be dealing with shirt introductions in many cases. But taking notes like this in lectures will give you some pointers for activity and discussion in the labs, and subsequently in your own time. We have time for discussion in the labs and through the online channels, through which we can explore the concepts, and issues introduced in the lectures.

So - take structured notes, and figure out a good way to do this so that you participate actively and effectively in lectures. Your teachers and classmates can advise you - and you can likely advise them.
